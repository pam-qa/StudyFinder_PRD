# 14. Glossary

[← Open Questions](13-open-questions.md) · [Home](../README.md)

Domain terms used across the PRD, in plain language.

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Clinical trial** | A research study in which people volunteer to test whether a treatment, device, or approach is safe and effective. |
| **Study team / coordinator** | The people running a trial at a site who screen, enroll, and support participants. |
| **Eligibility criteria** | The rules that decide who can join a study, split into **inclusion** (must have) and **exclusion** (must not have) criteria. |
| **Inclusion criteria** | Conditions a person must meet to be considered for a study. |
| **Exclusion criteria** | Conditions that disqualify a person from a study. |
| **Screening** | The study team's process of confirming whether someone actually qualifies. StudyFinder does **not** do this. |
| **Enrollment / accrual** | Signing participants into a trial. Slow accrual is a major reason trials are delayed. |
| **Phase** | The stage of a trial (e.g., Phase 1–4) reflecting its purpose and size. |
| **Recruiting status** | Whether a study is currently accepting participants (e.g., recruiting, not yet recruiting, completed). |
| **Sponsor** | The organization responsible for a trial. |
| **NCT number** | The unique ID ClinicalTrials.gov assigns to each registered study. |
| **ClinicalTrials.gov** | The U.S. NIH public registry of clinical studies; StudyFinder's primary data source. See [Integrations](10-integrations.md). |
| **Eligibility snapshot** | StudyFinder's plain-language, non-final estimate of whether a user may qualify (**may qualify / needs review / likely not a match**). |
| **PHI (Protected Health Information)** | Health data protected under HIPAA when handled by covered entities/business associates. See [Compliance](11-compliance-privacy-security.md). |
| **IRB (Institutional Review Board)** | A committee that reviews and oversees the ethics of a study. Out of scope for StudyFinder. |
| **Concept mapping** | Translating a user's free-text condition into standardized medical terms for reliable matching. |
| **False-hope rate** | Guardrail metric: how often a "may qualify" flag is later contradicted by the study team. See [Goals & Metrics](03-goals-and-metrics.md). |
